FAQs – I‑693 Medical Exam

What is the I‑693 medical exam?
The I‑693 medical exam is a USCIS‑required immigration medical examination used to determine whether an applicant meets U.S. immigration health and vaccination requirements.
Who needs to complete the I‑693 medical exam?
Anyone applying for adjustment of status (green card) or instructed by USCIS must complete the I‑693 medical exam.
Who is authorized to perform the I‑693 medical exam?
The I‑693 medical exam must be completed by a USCIS-certified surgeon, also known as a USCIS civil surgeon.
What does the I‑693 medical exam include?
The I‑693 medical exam includes a medical history review, physical examination, required tests, and verification of immigration vaccination requirements.
What documents are required for the I‑693 medical exam?
Applicants typically need a government‑issued photo ID, vaccination records, and any USCIS instructions related to Form I‑693
What are the vaccination requirements for the I‑693 medical exam?
Vaccination requirements are set by USCIS and may include vaccines such as MMR, Tdap, influenza, and others based on age and medical history.
What is the sealed envelope for the I‑693 medical exam?
After completion, the USCIS - certified surgeon. seals the I‑693 form in an envelope, which must remain unopened when submitted to USCIS.
How long is the I‑693 medical exam valid?
The validity of the I‑693 medical exam depends on current USCIS rules and the timing of your immigration application submission.
How much does the I‑693 medical exam cost?
The cost of the I‑693 medical exam varies by clinic and location and is determined by the USCIS ‑certified surgeon. performing the exam.
How does easyIME help with the I‑693 medical exam?
easyIME helps applicants find USCIS-certified surgeons, book appointments online, and navigate the I‑693 medical exam process accurately and securely.
